# One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) -- Complete Tax Provisions Reference
**IRC Section(s):** Multiple (see individual provisions below)
**Relevant Forms:** Form 1040, Schedule 1-A, Schedule A, Form 4547, Form 8995, Form 4562, Form 706, Form 709
**Tax Years Covered:** 2025-2026+
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Overview

Public Law 119-21, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), was signed into law on July 4, 2025. The legislation permanently extends and modifies key provisions of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA, P.L. 115-97) that were scheduled to sunset after December 31, 2025, introduces new temporary deductions for tips, overtime, auto loan interest, and seniors, creates Trump Accounts (Section 530A) as a new savings vehicle for children, terminates several clean energy credits enacted under the Inflation Reduction Act, and enacts a tiered endowment excise tax and a remittance transfer excise tax. This document is the master reference for all OBBBA tax provisions organized by permanence, temporality, and repeal status.

Sources: Congress.gov (P.L. 119-21 full text), IRS.gov (FS-2025-03, IR-2025-86, Notice 2026-11), Tax Foundation, Baker Tilly, Katten, Grant Thornton, RSM, AICPA-CIMA, CRS Report R48631.

## PART I: PERMANENT CHANGES

### 1. Individual Income Tax Brackets (Section 1)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** TCJA rates (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%) through 2025. Scheduled to revert to pre-TCJA levels (top rate 39.6%) on January 1, 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Seven-bracket structure made permanent. Top rate stays 37%. For 2026, the 10% and 12% brackets receive an additional inflation adjustment, widening income taxed at the lowest rates.

**Effective date:** Permanent for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. See 00-ordinary-income-brackets.md for full bracket tables.

### 2. Standard Deduction Increased and Permanent (Section 63)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA nearly doubled the standard deduction through 2025 ($15,000 Single / $30,000 MFJ in 2025 pre-OBBBA adjustment). Set to revert to approximately half those amounts in 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The increased standard deduction is made permanent and further raised. Indexed for inflation annually.

| Filing Status | 2025 | 2026 |
|---------------|------|------|
| Single / MFS | $15,750 | $16,000 |
| MFJ / Surviving Spouse | $31,500 | $32,000 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 | $24,000 (est.) |

Additional standard deduction for age 65+ or blind: $2,050 (single/HOH in 2026); $1,650 per qualifying spouse (MFJ in 2026). These amounts are separate from the new $6,000 senior deduction (see Part II).

### 3. Personal Exemption Permanently Eliminated (Section 151)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA suspended the personal exemption deduction ($0 value) through 2025. Scheduled to return at approximately $5,300 per exemption in 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The personal exemption is permanently eliminated. The exemption amount remains at $0 for all future tax years.

**Effective date:** Permanent for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025.

### 4. Child Tax Credit -- $2,200 with Inflation Indexing (Section 24)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA increased the CTC from $1,000 to $2,000 per qualifying child through 2025. Scheduled to revert to $1,000 in 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The maximum CTC is permanently set at $2,200 per qualifying child with annual inflation indexing. The refundable portion remains at $1,700 for 2026. The phase-out threshold for MFJ is reduced from $400,000 to $200,000 starting in 2026.

**Effective date:** 2025 for the $2,200 amount; inflation indexing begins 2026.

### 5. Section 199A QBI Deduction Permanent with $400 Minimum (Section 199A)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA created a 20% deduction for qualified business income through pass-through entities, expiring after December 31, 2025.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The 20% QBI deduction is made permanent. A new minimum deduction of $400 applies for taxpayers with at least $1,000 of active QBI (both figures indexed for inflation beginning 2027). The phase-in range for wage/capital and SSTB limitations is expanded from $50,000/$100,000 above the threshold to $75,000/$150,000, providing a wider band before full limitations apply. No changes to QBI calculations take effect until 2026.

**Effective date:** Permanent for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025.

### 6. 100% Bonus Depreciation Permanent (Section 168(k))

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** TCJA provided 100% bonus depreciation 2018-2022, then phased down 20%/year (40% in 2025, 0% by 2027).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** 100% bonus depreciation permanently restored for qualified property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025. Covers tangible property with recovery period of 20 years or less. No dollar cap, no income limitation. Written binding contracts before January 20, 2025, lock the acquisition date (may disqualify). A 40% election available in lieu of full expensing for the first tax year ending after January 19, 2025.

**Effective date:** Retroactive to property acquired after January 19, 2025. Permanent.

### 7. Section 179 Doubled to $2,500,000 (Section 179)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA set the Section 179 deduction at $1,000,000 with a $2,500,000 phase-out threshold (both indexed for inflation; $1,250,000 / $3,130,000 for 2025).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The Section 179 maximum deduction is doubled to $2,500,000 with a phase-out threshold of $4,000,000 for property placed in service in tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Both amounts are indexed for inflation. Taxable income limitation and carry-forward rules continue to apply.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Permanent.

### 8. Section 163(j) EBITDA Calculation Restored (Section 163(j))

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Business interest limited to 30% of ATI. ATI used EBITDA basis 2018-2021, shifted to less favorable EBIT basis (no D&A addbacks) for 2022+.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** EBITDA-based ATI permanently restored for tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. D&A addbacks return. Floor-plan financing expanded to include trailers/campers. Certain international items (Subpart F, CFC tested income, Section 78 gross-up) excluded from ATI starting 2026.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Permanent.

### 9. Section 174A Domestic R&E Immediate Expensing (Section 174A -- new)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA required capitalization and 5-year amortization of domestic R&E expenditures (15 years for foreign R&E) starting in 2022. This was a significant change from decades of immediate expensing.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** New Section 174A permanently allows full expensing of domestic R&E expenditures in the year paid or incurred for tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Taxpayers may alternatively elect to capitalize and amortize over a minimum of 60 months. Unamortized domestic R&E from 2022-2024 may be accelerated (fully deducted in 2025 or ratably over 2 years). Foreign R&E remains subject to 15-year amortization.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Permanent.

### 10. Estate/Gift Exemption $15,000,000 Permanent (Sections 2010, 2505)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA doubled the unified estate and gift tax exemption to $10,000,000 (indexed; $13,990,000 for 2025). Scheduled to revert to approximately $7,000,000 in 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The unified exemption is permanently set at $15,000,000 per individual ($30,000,000 for married couples via portability) beginning January 1, 2026. Indexed for inflation beginning 2027 (using 2025 as base year). The generation-skipping transfer (GST) tax exemption matches the estate/gift exemption. The top estate and gift tax rate remains 40%.

**Effective date:** Calendar year 2026 and thereafter. Permanent.

### 11. Section 461(l) Excess Business Loss Limitation Permanent (Section 461(l))

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA limited excess business losses for noncorporate taxpayers (losses exceeding approximately $500,000 MFJ, indexed) through 2028. Excess losses were treated as net operating loss carryforwards. Scheduled to expire after 2028.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The excess business loss limitation is made permanent. The threshold is adjusted by clawing back eight years of inflation adjustments, effectively lowering the dollar amount at which the limitation applies. Disallowed losses continue to be treated as NOL carryforwards.

**Effective date:** Permanent, no sunset.

### 12. AMT Exemptions Preserved; Phase-Out Rate Doubled (Section 55)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA increased AMT exemption amounts and phase-out thresholds through 2025. The exemption phase-out rate was 25% of AMTI exceeding the threshold. Scheduled to revert to pre-TCJA levels in 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** AMT exemptions are made permanent. Phase-out thresholds revert to $500,000 (Single/HOH) / $1,000,000 (MFJ), indexed for inflation starting 2026. The phase-out rate doubles from 25% to 50% of AMTI exceeding the threshold, accelerating the phase-out and increasing the number of high-income filers subject to AMT.

| AMT Parameter | 2025 | 2026 (OBBBA) |
|---------------|------|-------------|
| Exemption (MFJ) | $137,000 | ~$137,000 (indexed) |
| Exemption (Single/HOH) | $88,100 | ~$88,100 (indexed) |
| Phase-out threshold (MFJ) | $1,252,700 | $1,000,000 (indexed from 2027) |
| Phase-out rate | 25% | 50% |

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 13. Mortgage Interest Cap at $750,000 Permanent (Section 163(h))

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA reduced the mortgage interest deduction limit from $1,000,000 to $750,000 of acquisition indebtedness ($375,000 MFS) for mortgages originated after December 15, 2017, and suspended the deduction for home equity indebtedness (previously $100,000). These changes were temporary through 2025.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The $750,000 acquisition indebtedness limit and the suspension of home equity interest deduction are made permanent. Certain mortgage insurance premiums are included as qualified residence interest.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 14. 60% AGI Charitable Contribution Limit Permanent (Section 170)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA increased the AGI limit for cash charitable contributions from 50% to 60% through 2025. Scheduled to revert to 50%.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The 60% AGI limit for cash contributions is made permanent. A new 0.5% AGI floor applies to itemized charitable deductions beginning in 2026 -- contributions below this floor are not deductible and cannot be carried forward. For taxpayers in the 37% bracket, the tax benefit of itemized deductions (including charitable) is capped at the 35% rate (the "2/37 rule"), creating a 2% haircut on the value of deductions. Non-cash contribution limit remains at 50% of AGI.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 15. Section 1202 QSBS Cap Raised to $15,000,000; Graduated Holding Period (Section 1202)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** For QSBS acquired after September 27, 2010, a noncorporate taxpayer could exclude 100% of gain on stock held more than 5 years, up to the greater of $10,000,000 or 10x basis. The issuing corporation's gross assets could not exceed $50,000,000.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** For QSBS issued after July 4, 2025: the per-issuer gain exclusion cap increases to $15,000,000 (indexed for inflation starting 2027); the gross asset threshold increases to $75,000,000 (indexed starting 2027); and a graduated exclusion schedule replaces the 5-year cliff: 3-year hold = 50% exclusion, 4-year hold = 75% exclusion, 5+ years = 100% exclusion. The 10x basis alternative is unchanged. Gain not excluded at the 3-year or 4-year tier is taxed at a 28% capital gains rate.

**Effective date:** QSBS issued after July 4, 2025. Permanent.

### 16. Qualified Opportunity Zone Program Permanent with Rural Provisions (Section 1400Z)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** QOZ program provided capital gain deferral, step-up in basis (10% at 5 years, 15% at 7 years), and permanent exclusion after 10 years. Scheduled to expire.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** QOZ program made permanent. Existing designations remain until December 31, 2028; new tracts proposed every 10 years. From January 1, 2027: deferral for gains reinvested within 180 days; gains taxable at earlier of sale or 5th anniversary; 10% step-up after 5 years (no 7-year step-up); 10-year gains permanently excluded; 30-year basis stepped to FMV. Qualified Rural Opportunity Funds (QROFs) created with reduced 50% basis improvement requirement.

**Effective date:** Permanent. New incentive structure effective January 1, 2027.

### 17. Moving Expense Deduction Permanently Repealed (Section 217)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA suspended the moving expense deduction and employer-provided moving expense exclusion for 2018-2025, retaining the exception for active-duty members of the Armed Forces and intelligence community.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The repeal is made permanent. The military and intelligence community exception is retained.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 18. Section 48D Semiconductor Credit Increased to 35% (Section 48D)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The CHIPS Act provided a 25% investment tax credit for semiconductor and semiconductor equipment manufacturing facilities, expiring after December 31, 2026.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The credit rate increases from 25% to 35% for property placed in service after December 31, 2025. The termination date remains December 31, 2026. This primarily benefits projects already under construction.

**Effective date:** Property placed in service after December 31, 2025 through December 31, 2026.

### 19. Miscellaneous Itemized Deductions Permanently Eliminated

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA suspended miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the 2% AGI floor (including investment advisory fees, tax preparation fees, unreimbursed employee expenses) through 2025.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The suspension is made permanent. These deductions are no longer available.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

## PART II: TEMPORARY CHANGES (WITH SUNSET DATES)

### 20. SALT Cap Raised to $40,000 (2025-2029) (Section 164)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** TCJA capped SALT deduction at $10,000 ($5,000 MFS) for 2018-2025.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** SALT cap increases to $40,000 ($20,000 MFS) for 2025, rising 1% annually through 2029. Phase-down: cap reduced by $0.30 per dollar of MAGI above $500,000 ($250,000 MFS), floor of $10,000. PTET workaround remains available.

| Year | Cap | MFS | Phase-Down Start | Full Phase-Down At |
|------|-----|-----|------------------|--------------------|
| 2025 | $40,000 | $20,000 | $500,000 | $600,000 |
| 2026 | $40,400 | $20,200 | $505,000 | ~$606,333 |
| 2027 | $40,804 | $20,402 | $510,050 | ~$612,730 |
| 2028 | $41,212 | $20,606 | $515,150 | ~$619,190 |
| 2029 | $41,624 | $20,812 | $520,302 | ~$625,715 |
| 2030+ | $10,000 | $5,000 | N/A | N/A |

**Sunset:** Reverts to $10,000 for tax years beginning after December 31, 2029.

### 21. Tip Income Deduction -- Up to $25,000 (2025-2028)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** All tip income was fully taxable for federal income tax purposes.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Taxpayers in occupations that customarily received tips before 2025 may claim an above-the-line deduction of up to $25,000 of qualified tip income annually. Available to both itemizers and non-itemizers. Phases out when MAGI exceeds $150,000 (Single) / $300,000 (MFJ). Reported on Schedule 1-A. Payroll taxes (Social Security and Medicare) still apply to tip income.

**Sunset:** Expires after December 31, 2028.

### 22. Overtime Premium Pay Deduction -- Up to $12,500 (2025-2028)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** All overtime compensation was fully taxable for federal income tax purposes.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The premium portion of overtime pay (the amount exceeding the regular rate, e.g., the "half" of time-and-a-half) may be deducted above-the-line up to $12,500 per individual ($25,000 MFJ). Must be overtime required under the FLSA. Phases out when MAGI exceeds $150,000 (Single) / $300,000 (MFJ). Reported on Schedule 1-A.

**Sunset:** Expires after December 31, 2028.

### 23. Auto Loan Interest Deduction -- Up to $10,000 (2025-2028)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Personal auto loan interest was not deductible (personal interest has been nondeductible since 1986 TRA).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Up to $10,000 of interest on a qualified passenger vehicle loan may be deducted above-the-line. The vehicle must be new (original use begins with the taxpayer), purchased for personal use, have final assembly in the United States, and the loan must be originated after December 31, 2024, and secured by a lien on the vehicle. Leases do not qualify. Phases out when MAGI exceeds $100,000 (Single) / $200,000 (MFJ). Reported on Schedule 1-A.

**Sunset:** Expires after December 31, 2028.

### 24. Senior Deduction -- $6,000 for Age 65+ (2025-2028) (Section 151)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** No separate above-the-line deduction for seniors existed beyond the additional standard deduction for age 65+.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Taxpayers age 65 or older may claim an additional $6,000 deduction ($12,000 for couples where both qualify). This is separate from and in addition to the existing additional standard deduction for seniors. Available to both itemizers and non-itemizers. Phases out at a 6% rate when MAGI exceeds $75,000 (Single) / $150,000 (MFJ). MFS filers are not eligible. Reported on Schedule 1-A.

**Sunset:** Expires after December 31, 2028.

## PART III: NEW PROGRAMS

### 25. Trump Accounts (Section 530A -- new)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** No comparable account type existed.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** New IRA-type account for children under 18 with SSN. Annual limit: $5,000 (indexed after 2027), after-tax/non-deductible. Government seed: $1,000 for births 2025-2028 (does not count against limit). Employer contributions up to $2,500/employee under Section 128 (counts toward $5,000). Contributions begin July 4, 2026. Tax-deferred growth; distributions available at age 18, taxed as ordinary income (minus basis). 10% early withdrawal penalty before 59-1/2 (exceptions for education, first home). Election via Form 4547 or trumpaccounts.gov.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. No contributions before July 4, 2026.

### 26. Non-Itemizer Charitable Deduction Reinstated (Section 170)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** No above-the-line charitable deduction was available after 2021 (temporary COVID-era deduction of $300/$600 expired).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Taxpayers who take the standard deduction may permanently deduct up to $1,000 (Single) / $2,000 (MFJ) in qualified cash charitable contributions above the line. Not subject to the 0.5% AGI floor that applies to itemizers. Contributions to donor-advised funds, private foundations, and supporting organizations are excluded.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 27. Remittance Excise Tax -- 1% (Section 4475 -- new)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** No federal excise tax on outbound remittance transfers existed.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** A 1% excise tax is imposed on certain cross-border money transfers from the U.S. to foreign countries. The tax is calculated on the transfer amount (not fees) and collected by the remittance provider. Exemptions include transfers from accounts at BSA-subject financial institutions (banks, credit unions, investment companies), transfers funded by U.S.-issued debit or credit cards, and certain cryptocurrency transfers. U.S. citizens may be eligible for a credit or refund with proper documentation.

**Effective date:** January 1, 2026.

### 28. Tiered Endowment Excise Tax -- Up to 8% (Section 4968)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The TCJA imposed a flat 1.4% excise tax on net investment income of private colleges and universities with 500+ students and per-student endowments exceeding $500,000.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The tax is restructured into a tiered system based on per-student endowment. The minimum student threshold increases from 500 to 3,000 tuition-paying students. Net investment income definition expands to include student loan interest and certain research royalties.

| Student-Adjusted Endowment | Excise Tax Rate |
|----------------------------|-----------------|
| $500,000 - $750,000 | 1.4% |
| $750,001 - $2,000,000 | 4% |
| Over $2,000,000 | 8% |

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025.

## PART IV: REPEALED / TERMINATED PROVISIONS

### 29. Section 30D New Clean Vehicle Credit -- Terminated

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The IRA provided up to $7,500 for the purchase of new qualifying clean vehicles through 2032.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Credit terminated for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

### 30. Section 25E Used Clean Vehicle Credit -- Terminated

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The IRA provided up to $4,000 for previously owned clean vehicles through 2032.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Credit terminated for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

### 31. Section 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit -- Expired

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The IRA provided a 30% credit (up to $1,200/$2,000 depending on improvement type) for energy-efficient home improvements through 2032.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Credit terminated for property placed in service after December 31, 2025.

### 32. Section 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit -- Expired

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The IRA provided a 30% credit for residential solar, wind, geothermal, and biomass systems through 2034.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Credit terminated for expenditures made after December 31, 2025. Installation must be completed by year-end to qualify.

### 33. Section 45W Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit -- Terminated

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Up to $7,500 (light-duty) / $40,000 (heavy-duty) for commercial clean vehicles through 2032.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Credit terminated for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

## PART V: OTHER NOTABLE PROVISIONS

### 34. Dependent Care FSA -- Raised to $7,500 (Section 129)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The dependent care assistance program exclusion was $5,000 ($2,500 MFS).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Increased to $7,500 ($3,750 MFS) beginning in 2026. Not indexed for inflation. Permanent.

### 35. Adoption Credit -- Partially Refundable

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The adoption tax credit (up to $16,810 for 2024) was nonrefundable.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The adoption credit is now fully refundable with increased maximum amounts ($17,280 for 2025). Phase-out begins at $250,000 MAGI, fully phased out at $290,000 (indexed for inflation).

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2024.

### 36. Charitable Deduction -- 0.5% AGI Floor for Itemizers; Benefit Capped at 35% Rate

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** No AGI floor on charitable deductions for individuals. Full tax benefit at the taxpayer's marginal rate.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Starting in 2026, itemizers cannot deduct charitable contributions that do not exceed 0.5% of AGI. For taxpayers in the 37% bracket, the tax benefit of all itemized deductions is capped at the 35% rate (the "2/37 rule"). Amounts below the 0.5% floor cannot be carried forward.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. Permanent.

### 37. Saver's Credit Converts to Matching Contribution (Sections 25B, 6433)

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Nonrefundable Saver's Credit of up to $1,000 ($2,000 MFJ).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA / SECURE 2.0):** Starting 2027, replaced by Saver's Match: 50% federal match on contributions up to $2,000 (max $1,000), deposited directly into participant's retirement account. Gradual MAGI phase-out. Available for 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), IRA.

**Effective date:** Tax years beginning after December 31, 2026.

### 38. 1099-K Reporting Threshold -- $20,000 / 200 Transactions

**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** The American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 lowered the 1099-K threshold from $20,000/200 transactions to $600/no transaction minimum, but the IRS repeatedly delayed implementation (using $5,000 transitional threshold for 2024).

**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** The threshold is permanently set at $20,000 in aggregate payments AND 200 or more transactions. This effectively restores the pre-ARPA threshold.

**Effective date:** Permanent for returns filed after enactment.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE -- SUNSET TRACKING:** Verify tax year before applying temporary provisions. SALT cap: 2025-2029. Tip/overtime/auto/senior deductions: 2025-2028. Flag 2029+ computations as requiring reversal.

> **AGENT RULE -- BONUS DEPRECIATION DATE:** Check for written binding contract before January 20, 2025. If yes, apply TCJA phase-down (40% for 2025, 20% for 2026), not 100% OBBBA rate.

> **AGENT RULE -- SALT PHASE-DOWN:** Formula: Effective Cap = MAX($10,000, $40,000 - (0.30 x (MAGI - $500,000))). Full phase-down at ~$600,000 MAGI (2025). Adjust cap and threshold by 1% annually through 2029.

> **AGENT RULE -- QBI MINIMUM:** For 2026+, QBI deduction = GREATER of 20% calculation or $400 minimum (requires $1,000+ active QBI). Both indexed from 2027.

> **AGENT RULE -- ESTATE TIMING:** $15M exemption effective January 1, 2026. 2025 gifts use $13,990,000 exemption.

> **AGENT RULE -- EV/ENERGY CREDIT CUTOFF:** Clean vehicles (30D, 25E, 45W): terminated September 30, 2025. Residential energy (25C, 25D): terminated December 31, 2025. Check placed-in-service date.

> **AGENT RULE -- TRUMP ACCOUNTS:** No contributions before July 4, 2026. Government seed ($1,000) only for births 2025-2028. Election on Form 4547 or trumpaccounts.gov.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP -- SALT TORPEDO:** At $600,000+ MAGI (2025), the effective SALT cap phases down to $10,000 -- identical to the TCJA cap. Always compute the phase-down before advising on itemization.

> **TRAP -- BONUS DEPRECIATION BINDING CONTRACT:** Written binding contracts entered before January 20, 2025, lock the acquisition date. 100% OBBBA rate unavailable even if placed in service after that date. Apply TCJA phase-down.

> **TRAP -- TIP/OVERTIME vs. PAYROLL TAX:** These deductions reduce income tax only. Social Security and Medicare taxes still apply to full tip and overtime amounts.

> **TRAP -- AUTO LOAN REQUIREMENTS:** Vehicle must be new, U.S.-assembled, personal use, first-lien secured. Leases do not qualify.

> **TRAP -- CHARITABLE FLOOR:** Starting 2026, first 0.5% of AGI in charitable giving is nondeductible for itemizers and cannot be carried forward. Consider bunching strategies.

> **TRAP -- AMT PHASE-OUT DOUBLED:** 50% phase-out rate (up from 25%) means AMT exemption vanishes twice as fast. May trigger unexpected AMT for high-income taxpayers.

> **TRAP -- QSBS DUAL REGIME:** Pre-7/4/2025 stock: $10M cap, $50M assets, 5-year cliff. Post-7/4/2025 stock: $15M cap, $75M assets, graduated holding. Verify issuance date before applying rules.

> **TRAP -- 174A TRANSITION:** Unamortized 2022-2024 domestic R&E can be accelerated via method change in 2025. Missing this election means continued 5-year amortization.

> **TRAP -- TRUMP ACCOUNT DEADLINE:** Contributions due by December 31 (not tax filing deadline). The $5,000 limit is aggregate from all sources including employer contributions.
