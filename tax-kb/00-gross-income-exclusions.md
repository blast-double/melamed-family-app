# Gross Income Exclusions (IRC Sections 101-140)
**IRC Section(s):** Sections 101, 102, 103, 104, 105, 106, 108, 119, 121, 127, 129, 132, 135, 139
**Relevant Forms:** Form 1040, Form 8815, Form 6251, Form 8824, Form 4797, Form 982, Schedule D
**Tax Years Covered:** 2025-2026
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Overview

IRC Sections 101 through 140 enumerate specific items excluded from gross income under Section 61. These exclusions cover life insurance proceeds, gifts, municipal bond interest, personal injury damages, employer-provided health and fringe benefits, cancellation of debt, home sale gains, educational assistance, and disaster relief payments. Each exclusion carries distinct conditions, dollar limits, and reporting requirements. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed into law in 2025, modified several of these exclusions effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025.

## Rules

### 1. Section 101 -- Life Insurance Death Benefits
- Death benefit proceeds paid by reason of the insured's death are excluded from gross income.
- **Accelerated death benefits** (Section 101(g)) for terminally or chronically ill insured are excluded.
- Interest earned on proceeds held by insurer after death is **taxable**.
- Transfer-for-value rule: if a policy is transferred for consideration, the exclusion is limited to consideration paid plus premiums, unless an exception applies.

### 2. Section 102 -- Gifts and Inheritances
- Property received as a gift or inheritance is excluded from the recipient's gross income.
- Income earned on gifted/inherited property (dividends, interest, rent) **is taxable**.
- Donor may owe gift tax if gifts exceed the annual exclusion ($19,000/donee for 2025 and 2026).
- Inherited property receives stepped-up basis to FMV at date of death (Section 1014).

### 3. Section 103 -- State and Local Bond Interest
- Interest on obligations of state and local governments (municipal bonds) is excluded from gross income.
- Private activity bond interest is an **AMT preference item** and must be reported on Form 6251.
- The exclusion does not extend to gains realized on the sale of municipal bonds.

### 4. Section 104 -- Compensation for Injuries or Sickness
- Damages received on account of **physical injury or physical sickness** are excluded (whether by suit or settlement).
- **Punitive damages** are never excluded, regardless of the nature of the underlying claim.
- **Emotional distress** damages are excluded only to the extent attributable to physical injury or physical sickness; otherwise taxable.
- **Workers' compensation** benefits for occupational injury or sickness are fully excluded.

### 5. Sections 105/106 -- Employer Health Plans
- Employer-paid health/accident insurance premiums (Section 106) are excluded from employee income.
- Employer HSA contributions excluded up to statutory limits (see Key Thresholds table).
- Reimbursements under employer health plans for Section 213(d) medical expenses (Section 105) are excluded.
- Wage replacement payments under employer-funded plans are **taxable**.

### 6. Section 108 -- Cancellation of Debt (COD) Income
- COD income is generally includable in gross income under Section 61(a)(11).
- Exclusions apply in these situations:
  - **Bankruptcy** (Title 11 case) -- full exclusion
  - **Insolvency** -- excluded to the extent the taxpayer is insolvent immediately before discharge
  - **Qualified farm indebtedness**
  - **Qualified real property business indebtedness (QRPBI)**
  - **Student loan forgiveness** for death or total/permanent disability (Section 108(f)(5)) -- made permanent by OBBBA
- When COD income is excluded, **attribute reduction** rules under Section 108(b) apply (reduce NOLs, credits, basis of property).
- Report exclusions on **Form 982**.

### 7. Section 119 -- Meals and Lodging for Convenience of Employer
- The value of meals furnished on the employer's business premises for the convenience of the employer is excluded.
- The value of lodging furnished on the employer's business premises is excluded if acceptance is a **required condition of employment**.
- Cash meal allowances do not qualify for the exclusion.

### 8. Section 121 -- Primary Residence Gain Exclusion
- Exclude up to **$250,000** (single) / **$500,000** (MFJ) of gain from sale of principal residence.
- **Ownership test:** owned 2 of last 5 years. **Use test:** principal residence 2 of last 5 years. 24 months need not be consecutive.
- For MFJ: both spouses meet use test; only one must meet ownership test.
- **Frequency:** once every 2 years.
- **Partial exclusion** for unforeseen circumstances (health, employment change) -- prorated by fraction of 2-year requirement met.
- **Non-qualified use (Section 121(b)(5)):** gain allocable to post-2008 periods of non-principal-residence use is not excludable. Ratio = non-qualified periods / total ownership. Exception: periods after last date of principal-residence use are not non-qualified.
- **Depreciation recapture:** unrecaptured Section 1250 gain is never excludable.
- **Surviving spouse:** $500,000 exclusion available if sale within 2 years of death and use test met before death.

### 9. Section 127 -- Employer Educational Assistance
- Employer-provided educational assistance up to **$5,250/year** is excluded from employee income.
- Covers undergraduate and graduate-level courses.
- Not limited to job-related education.
- OBBBA permanently extends inclusion of **employer student loan repayment assistance** under Section 127 (previously set to expire December 31, 2025).
- Beginning in tax years after 2026, the $5,250 limit will be adjusted for inflation.

### 10. Section 129 -- Dependent Care Assistance
- Employer-provided dependent care assistance is excluded up to annual limits through a qualified plan.
- The exclusion cannot exceed the lesser of the earned income of the employee (or spouse, if lower).

### 11. Section 132 -- Fringe Benefits
Excluded fringe benefit categories:
- **No-additional-cost services** -- services provided to employees at no substantial additional cost to the employer
- **Qualified employee discounts** -- up to gross profit percentage (goods) or 20% (services)
- **Working condition fringes** -- property/services that would be deductible as business expenses if paid by employee
- **De minimis fringes** -- property/services so small in value that accounting is unreasonable
- **Qualified transportation fringes (Section 132(f))** -- transit passes, commuter highway vehicle, and qualified parking
- **On-premises athletic facilities**
- **Qualified retirement planning services**

### 12. Section 135 -- U.S. Savings Bond Interest for Education
- Interest on Series EE/I bonds (issued after 1989) excluded if used for qualified higher education expenses.
- Owner must be 24+ at bond issue date. Exclusion reduced pro rata if proceeds exceed expenses.
- Phase-outs apply (see Key Thresholds). MFS filing status disqualifies. Report on **Form 8815**.

### 13. Section 139 -- Qualified Disaster Relief Payments
- Payments for reasonable/necessary personal, family, living, or funeral expenses from a qualified disaster are excluded.
- Any source qualifies (government, employer, charity). Does not cover insurance-compensated expenses.

## OBBBA Changes (Effective for Tax Years Beginning After December 31, 2025)

**Section 108(f)(5) -- Student Loan Discharge:**
**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Exclusion of student loan debt cancelled due to death or total/permanent disability was temporary (TCJA through December 31, 2025); ARPA temporarily broadened exclusion to all federal student loan cancellations through December 31, 2025.
**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Permanently extends the exclusion for student loans discharged on account of death or total/permanent disability. Does NOT extend the broader ARPA exclusion for IDR-plan forgiveness -- those discharges are taxable after 2025. Taxpayer's SSN must appear on the return for the year of discharge.

**Section 127 -- Employer Educational Assistance:**
**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Employer student loan repayment assistance under Section 127 was temporary (CARES Act through December 31, 2025).
**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Permanently extends tax-free employer student loan repayment assistance under Section 127. The $5,250 annual limit will be indexed for inflation beginning in tax years after 2026.

**Section 129 -- Dependent Care Assistance:**
**OLD RULE (pre-OBBBA):** Maximum annual exclusion was $5,000 ($2,500 MFS).
**NEW RULE (OBBBA):** Maximum annual exclusion increases to $7,500 ($3,750 MFS) for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** When a taxpayer receives something of value, first check Section 61 (is it gross income?), then check Sections 101-140 (is it specifically excluded?). Only amounts that are includable under Section 61 AND not excluded under Sections 101-140 enter the AGI calculation. Apply exclusions in order of specificity: named exclusion first, then general rules.

> **AGENT RULE:** For Section 121, verify all four conditions before applying the exclusion: (1) ownership test met, (2) use test met, (3) no other Section 121 exclusion claimed within prior 2 years, (4) no non-qualified use periods that reduce the excludable gain. If the taxpayer previously rented the property, compute the non-qualified use ratio and reduce excludable gain accordingly.

> **AGENT RULE:** For Section 108 COD exclusions, determine the ordering: bankruptcy exclusion applies first if taxpayer is in a Title 11 case; insolvency exclusion applies next to the extent of insolvency; then check qualified farm debt and QRPBI. Always apply attribute reduction under Section 108(b) after excluding COD income.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Section 121 non-qualified use -- if a taxpayer rents a home before converting to a primary residence, the portion of gain attributable to the rental period (post-2008) does NOT qualify for the Section 121 exclusion, even if the ownership and use tests are otherwise satisfied. The non-qualified use ratio is calculated as aggregate non-qualified periods divided by total ownership period, and that fraction of total gain is ineligible. Exception: rental periods AFTER the last date of principal-residence use within the 5-year lookback are NOT counted as non-qualified use.

> **TRAP:** Section 104 emotional distress -- damages for emotional distress are taxable unless the emotional distress originated from a physical injury or physical sickness. Medical expenses for emotional distress treatment may be excluded, but the remaining award is fully taxable. Do not assume "personal injury" means "physical injury."

> **TRAP:** Section 108 student loan post-OBBBA -- the OBBBA permanently excludes only student loan discharges due to death or total/permanent disability. Student loan forgiveness under Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) plans, Public Service Loan Forgiveness processed after 2025, or other federal programs is taxable income after December 31, 2025, unless the taxpayer qualifies for a separate exclusion (e.g., insolvency under Section 108(a)(1)(B)).

> **TRAP:** Section 103 AMT -- while municipal bond interest is excluded from regular income tax, private activity bond interest is an AMT preference item. Taxpayers subject to AMT must add this interest back when computing alternative minimum taxable income (Form 6251).
