# Forms Master Reference
**IRC Section(s):** Various
**Relevant Forms:** All listed below
**Tax Years Covered:** 2025-2026
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Overview

This capstone document provides an alphabetical/numerical reference for every IRS form, FinCEN report, and schedule relevant to individual and small-business tax compliance. For each form it lists the full name, purpose, who files, due date (calendar-year basis for TY 2025 returns filed in 2026 unless noted), the current e-file threshold, and the related knowledge-base document where substantive rules are covered. Due dates that fall on a weekend or legal holiday shift to the next business day; specific 2026-calendar adjusted dates are noted where applicable. The e-file threshold for information returns was lowered from 250 to 10 aggregate returns effective January 1, 2024 (T.D. 9972). The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed July 4, 2025, made several threshold and structural changes noted throughout.

**Sources:** IRS Publication 509 (2026); IRS General Instructions for Certain Information Returns (2025); IRS Instructions for Forms W-2 and W-3 (2026); IRS Form 1040-ES (2026); FinCEN Notice FIN-2025-NTC3; IRS Publication 1220 (Rev. 2-2026); IRS Instructions for Form 709 (2025); IRS FAQ on Estate Taxes (updated Dec. 2025); IRS Instructions for Form 1099-DA (2025); IRS Instructions for Form 7206 (2025); IRS Instructions for Form 8995 (2025).

## E-File Threshold Rule (Universal)

Effective for returns filed on or after January 1, 2024, any filer required to file **10 or more information returns** in aggregate across all form types (1097, 1098, 1099 series, 3921, 3922, 5498 series, W-2, W-2G, 1042-S, 1094/1095 series) must e-file all of them. Corrected returns are not counted toward the 10-return threshold but must be e-filed if originals were e-filed. A waiver may be requested on Form 8508 at least 45 days before the due date. Failure to e-file when required triggers penalties under IRC 6721, but only for the number of returns exceeding 10.

**OBBBA change (TY 2026+):** The 1099-NEC and 1099-MISC reporting threshold increases from $600 to $2,000 for payments made after December 31, 2025. The 1099-K TPSO threshold is permanently restored to $20,000 and 200+ transactions (retroactive to 2022).

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE — Form Selection:** When a taxpayer reports a transaction, identify the correct form by working backward from the income/deduction type. Rental income flows through Schedule E. Capital gains flow through Form 8949 then Schedule D. SE income flows through Schedule C then Schedule SE. Never assume a form is unnecessary; check the KB doc for the underlying substantive rules first.

> **AGENT RULE — Deadline Priority:** Partnership and S corporation returns (Form 1065, Form 1120-S) are due one month before individual returns. K-1s cannot be completed until entity returns are filed. Always process entity returns first (March 16) before individual returns (April 15). If entity returns are extended, flag that K-1s will not be available until September 15 and plan the individual extension accordingly.

> **AGENT RULE — E-File Threshold:** Count ALL information returns across ALL form types. If the combined total of W-2s, 1099s, 1098s, and other information returns is 10 or more, every return must be e-filed. This is an aggregate test, not a per-form-type test. For TY 2025 filings, this is mandatory.

> **AGENT RULE — FBAR vs. 8938:** These are separate filings with different agencies, thresholds, and deadlines. FinCEN 114 goes to FinCEN (Treasury) with a $10,000 aggregate threshold. Form 8938 goes to the IRS (attached to 1040) with thresholds that vary by filing status and residence. A taxpayer may need to file both. Never assume one replaces the other.

> **AGENT RULE — OBBBA Threshold Changes:** For TY 2025 (filing in 2026), the 1099-NEC and 1099-MISC threshold remains $600. The $2,000 threshold applies only to payments made after December 31, 2025 (TY 2026, filing in 2027). The 1099-K TPSO threshold of $20,000/200 transactions applies retroactively to TY 2022 and forward. Do not confuse these effective dates.

> **AGENT RULE — Form 1099-DA:** This form is new for TY 2025. It must be filed via IRIS (not FIRE). For TY 2025, only gross proceeds are reported. Starting TY 2026, cost basis is also required for covered securities (assets acquired on or after January 1, 2026, held continuously with the same broker). DeFi brokers are not required to file after the repeal of the DeFi broker regulations.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP — Missing K-1 Cascade:** If Form 1065 or 1120-S is filed late, all downstream individual returns depending on those K-1s will also be delayed. An entity extension does not automatically extend the individual's return. The individual must file their own Form 4868.

> **TRAP — FBAR Separate From Tax Return:** The FBAR (FinCEN 114) is filed electronically with FinCEN at bsaefiling.fincen.gov, not with the IRS. A tax return extension does not extend the FBAR deadline. The FBAR has its own automatic extension to October 15 with no form required.

> **TRAP — Form 3520 Paper Filing:** Form 3520 cannot be e-filed. It must be mailed to IRS, Ogden, UT. Penalties for late filing are 25% of the gross value of foreign trust transfers or 5% of trust assets per year for distribution reporting failures. There is no reasonable cause exception that applies automatically.

> **TRAP — W-3 Not Required for E-Filers:** If you e-file W-2s, do not also file a paper W-3. Filing both creates duplicate submissions and may trigger penalty notices.

> **TRAP — 1099-NEC Has No Extended E-File Deadline:** Unlike other 1099 forms that have a Feb. 28 (paper) / March 31 (e-file) split, Form 1099-NEC has a single deadline of January 31 (Feb. 2, 2026 for TY 2025). There is no automatic 30-day extension for e-filing.

> **TRAP — Form 706 Portability Election:** An estate below the filing threshold may still need to file Form 706 to make the portability election (transfer unused exclusion to surviving spouse). There is no threshold below which portability is automatic; the return must be filed.

> **TRAP — Form 8832 Timing:** An entity classification election is effective only if filed within 75 days before or after the requested effective date. Late elections require IRS relief procedures and are not guaranteed. Do not assume retroactive elections will be accepted.

> **TRAP — Estimated Tax Safe Harbor Mismatch:** Form 1040-ES quarterly payments must meet the lesser of 90% of current-year tax or 100% of prior-year tax (110% if prior-year AGI exceeds $150K) to avoid underpayment penalties. Missing even one quarterly payment can trigger the penalty even if the annual total is sufficient. See 10-penalties-interest.md.

> **TRAP — Form 982 Not Filed = COD Income Included:** If a taxpayer qualifies for an IRC 108 exclusion on canceled debt but fails to file Form 982, the exclusion is not automatic. The IRS will include the 1099-C amount as gross income without the Form 982 election.
