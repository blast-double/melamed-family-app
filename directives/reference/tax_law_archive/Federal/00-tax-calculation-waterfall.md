# Federal Tax Calculation Waterfall
**IRC Section(s):** Sections 1, 61, 62, 63, 67, 68, 199A, 1401, 1411, 3101
**Relevant Forms:** Form 1040, Schedule 1, Schedule 1-A, Schedule A, Schedule SE, Form 8995/8995-A, Form 6251, Form 8960, Form 8959
**Tax Years Covered:** 2025-2026
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Overview

Every individual federal income tax return follows the same sequential calculation: gross income, minus exclusions, minus adjustments, minus deductions, equals taxable income, times the applicable rate, minus credits, plus additional taxes, minus payments, equals tax due or refund. This document defines each step in that waterfall, the IRC authority behind it, the Form 1040 line where it appears, and the dollar thresholds for 2025 and 2026. Mastering this sequence is prerequisite to evaluating any individual tax question because credits, phase-outs, and surtaxes all depend on figures computed at earlier steps.

## Rules

### Step 1: Gross Income (IRC Section 61)

Section 61 defines gross income as "all income from whatever source derived." This includes wages, salaries, tips, business income, capital gains, rents, royalties, alimony received (pre-2019 agreements), retirement distributions, interest, dividends, Social Security benefits (taxable portion), unemployment compensation, gambling winnings, and any other accession to wealth. Gross income is the broadest measure of income before any reductions.

**Form 1040 Lines:** Lines 1 through 8 (individual income categories), summed on **Line 9** (Total Income).

### Step 2: Excluded Items (IRC Sections 101-140)

Certain items are statutorily excluded from gross income and never appear on Line 9. Key exclusions include life insurance proceeds (Section 101), gifts and inheritances (Section 102), interest on state/local bonds (Section 103), qualified scholarships (Section 117), certain foreign earned income (Section 911), and employer-provided health insurance (Section 106). Excluded items reduce gross income before it reaches Form 1040.

### Step 3: Above-the-Line Deductions (IRC Section 62 / Schedule 1 Part II)

Section 62 defines "adjusted gross income" as gross income minus specific deductions enumerated in Section 62(a). These are called "above-the-line" deductions because they reduce income before the AGI line. They are available regardless of whether the taxpayer itemizes.

**Form 1040:** Schedule 1, Part II, Lines 11-26 flow to Form 1040 **Line 10** (Adjustments to Income).

**Complete list of above-the-line deductions:**

| Deduction | IRC Section | 2025 Limit | Schedule 1 Line |
|-----------|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Educator expenses | 62(a)(2)(D) | $300 ($600 MFJ if both educators) | Line 11 |
| Certain business expenses (reservists, performing artists, fee-basis officials) | 62(a)(2)(A-C) | Varies | Line 12 |
| HSA contributions | 223 | Self-only: $4,300 (2025), $4,400 (2026); Family: $8,550 (2025), $8,750 (2026) | Line 13 |
| Moving expenses (military only, post-TCJA/OBBBA) | 217 | Actual expenses | Line 14 |
| 50% of self-employment tax | 164(f) / 1402 | 50% of SE tax computed on Schedule SE | Line 15 |
| Self-employed SEP/SIMPLE/qualified plan contributions | 62(a)(6) | Lesser of 25% of net SE income or $70,000 (2025) | Line 16 |
| Self-employed health insurance | 162(l) | 100% of premiums, limited to net SE income | Line 17 |
| Penalty on early withdrawal of savings | 62(a)(9) | Actual penalty amount | Line 18 |
| Alimony paid (pre-2019 divorce/separation agreements only) | 62(a)(10) / 215 | Actual amount paid | Line 19a |

### Step 4: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

**Form 1040 Line 11.** Total Income (Line 9) minus Adjustments to Income (Line 10) equals AGI.

### Step 5: Standard Deduction or Itemized Deductions (IRC Section 63 / Schedule A)

The taxpayer claims the **greater of** the standard deduction or itemized deductions (Schedule A). The OBBBA made the TCJA's higher standard deduction permanent and increased it slightly for 2025.

**Standard Deduction Amounts:**

| Filing Status | 2025 | 2026 |
|--------------|------|------|
| Single | $15,750 | $16,100 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $31,500 | $32,200 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 | $24,150 |
| Married Filing Separately | $15,750 | $16,100 |

**Additional standard deduction (age 65+ or blind):** $2,000 per qualifying condition for single/HoH (2025); $1,600 per qualifying condition per spouse for MFJ (2025).

**Form 1040 Line 12:** Standard deduction or Schedule A total.

### Step 6: OBBBA Additional Deductions (Schedule 1-A / Form 1040 Line 13b)

The OBBBA created four new temporary deductions for tax years 2025-2028, reported on Schedule 1-A. These are **below-the-line** deductions -- they reduce taxable income but do NOT reduce AGI. They are available regardless of whether the taxpayer itemizes or takes the standard deduction.

| Deduction | IRC Section | Cap | MAGI Phase-out Start | Phase-out Rate | Sunset |
|-----------|------------|-----|---------------------|---------------|--------|
| Qualified tip income | 224 | $25,000 | $150,000 (S) / $300,000 (MFJ) | $100 per $1,000 over threshold (10%) | After 2028 |
| Qualified overtime compensation | 225 | $12,500 (S) / $25,000 (MFJ) | $150,000 (S) / $300,000 (MFJ) | $100 per $1,000 over threshold (10%) | After 2028 |
| Auto loan interest (new U.S.-assembled vehicles) | 163(h)(4) | $10,000 | $100,000 (S) / $200,000 (MFJ) | $200 per $1,000 over threshold (20%) | After 2028 |
| Senior deduction (age 65+) | OBBBA Sec. 70401 | $6,000 per person ($12,000 MFJ) | $75,000 (S) / $150,000 (MFJ) | $60 per $1,000 over threshold (6%) | After 2028 |

**Form 1040 Line 13b:** Total from Schedule 1-A.

### Step 7: Qualified Business Income Deduction (IRC Section 199A)

Up to 20% of qualified business income from pass-through entities (sole proprietorships, partnerships, S corporations). The OBBBA made the Section 199A deduction permanent (it was set to expire after 2025 under TCJA). This is a below-the-line deduction; it does not reduce AGI.

**Form 1040 Line 13a:** From Form 8995 (simplified) or Form 8995-A (standard).

### Step 8: Taxable Income

**Form 1040 Line 15.** AGI (Line 11) minus standard/itemized deductions (Line 12) minus Schedule 1-A deductions (Line 13b) minus QBI deduction (Line 13a) equals taxable income. Taxable income cannot be less than zero.

### Step 9: Tax on Taxable Income

Apply the applicable rate schedule to taxable income:

- **Ordinary income:** Seven brackets (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%) per IRC Section 1 and the rate tables. The OBBBA made the TCJA bracket structure permanent.
- **Long-term capital gains and qualified dividends:** Preferential rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on taxable income thresholds.
- **Net investment income tax (NIIT):** 3.8% on the lesser of net investment income or MAGI exceeding $200,000 (S) / $250,000 (MFJ) -- Section 1411, Form 8960.

**Form 1040 Line 16:** Tax (from Tax Table, Tax Computation Worksheet, or Schedule D Tax Worksheet).

### Step 10: Nonrefundable Credits

Nonrefundable credits reduce regular tax liability to zero but not below. Key credits include:

- Child Tax Credit (nonrefundable portion) -- up to $2,000 per qualifying child (OBBBA increased to $2,500 for 2025-2028)
- Credit for Other Dependents -- $500 per qualifying dependent
- Education credits (Lifetime Learning Credit)
- Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)
- Retirement savings contributions credit (Saver's Credit)
- Residential energy credits (repealed for most after 2025 by OBBBA)

**Form 1040 Line 21:** Total nonrefundable credits from Schedule 3.

### Step 11: Additional Taxes (Schedule 2 / Form 1040 Line 23)

| Tax | IRC Section | Rate / Threshold | Form |
|-----|------------|-----------------|------|
| Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) | 55-59 | 26%/28% on AMTI exceeding exemption ($88,100 S / $137,000 MFJ in 2025) | Form 6251 |
| Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) | 1411 | 3.8% on lesser of NII or MAGI over $200,000 (S) / $250,000 (MFJ) | Form 8960 |
| Additional Medicare Tax | 3101(b)(2) | 0.9% on wages/SE income over $200,000 (S) / $250,000 (MFJ) | Form 8959 |
| Self-Employment Tax | 1401 | 15.3% (12.4% OASDI up to $176,100 in 2025 + 2.9% Medicare, uncapped) | Schedule SE |
| Household employment taxes | 3510 | Varies | Schedule H |
| Recapture taxes | Various | Varies | Form 8828, etc. |

**Note:** 50% of self-employment tax is deductible above-the-line (Step 3). The full SE tax is reported on Schedule 2.

### Step 12: Refundable Credits

Refundable credits can reduce total tax below zero, generating a refund. Key refundable credits:

- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) -- max $8,046 (2025) with 3+ qualifying children
- Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) -- refundable portion of CTC
- Premium Tax Credit (PTC) -- Form 8962
- American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC) -- 40% refundable, up to $1,000

**Form 1040 Line 27:** Total refundable credits.

### Step 13: Payments

- Federal income tax withheld from W-2 box 2, 1099 withholding (Form 1040 **Line 25**)
- Estimated tax payments via Form 1040-ES (Form 1040 **Line 26**)
- Prior-year overpayment applied (included in Line 26)
- Amount paid with extension (Form 4868)

### Step 14: Tax Due or Refund

**Form 1040 Line 37:** Total tax (Line 24) minus total payments and credits (Line 33). Positive = tax due. Negative = refund (or applied to next year's estimated tax).

## OBBBA Changes (if applicable)

**OLD RULE (pre-2025):** The TCJA provisions -- including the higher standard deduction, seven-bracket rate structure, elimination of personal exemptions, and the Section 199A QBI deduction -- were scheduled to sunset after December 31, 2025, reverting to pre-2017 law with lower standard deductions, higher rates, and restored personal exemptions. Tip income, overtime pay, and auto loan interest had no special deductions. No separate senior deduction existed beyond the additional standard deduction for age 65+.

**NEW RULE (OBBBA, signed July 4, 2025):** The TCJA individual tax provisions were made permanent. The standard deduction was slightly increased for 2025 ($750 more for single, $1,500 more for MFJ versus the originally scheduled 2025 amounts). Four new temporary deductions (2025-2028) were created on Schedule 1-A: qualified tips ($25,000 cap), qualified overtime ($12,500/$25,000 cap), auto loan interest ($10,000 cap), and senior deduction ($6,000/$12,000). The Section 199A QBI deduction was made permanent. The SALT cap was raised from $10,000 to $40,000 for 2025-2029. All four Schedule 1-A deductions sunset after 2028 unless extended by Congress.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** AGI (Form 1040, Line 11) is the single most important number on any individual tax return. Dozens of phase-outs, limitations, and credit calculations reference AGI or MAGI. Always compute AGI before evaluating any credit eligibility, itemized deduction limitation, or surtax applicability. When building a tax projection, lock AGI first, then cascade downstream calculations from it.

> **AGENT RULE:** The four OBBBA Schedule 1-A deductions (tips, overtime, auto loan interest, senior) are below-the-line deductions entered on Form 1040 Line 13b. They reduce taxable income but do NOT reduce AGI. Do not include them in AGI calculations. Do not confuse them with the Section 62 above-the-line deductions on Schedule 1 Part II.

> **AGENT RULE:** When the waterfall calls for "minus deductions," apply them in this exact order: (1) standard or itemized deductions (Line 12), then (2) Schedule 1-A additional deductions (Line 13b), then (3) QBI deduction (Line 13a). The sum of all three is subtracted from AGI to reach taxable income on Line 15.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** MAGI is not always the same as AGI. Different provisions use different MAGI definitions. Roth IRA MAGI adds back student loan interest deduction and IRA deduction to AGI. ACA Premium Tax Credit MAGI adds back tax-exempt interest and excluded foreign income. The OBBBA Schedule 1-A phase-outs use MAGI that adds back foreign earned income exclusions and certain U.S. territory exclusions. Always use the MAGI definition specific to the provision being applied.

> **TRAP:** The OBBBA tip and overtime deductions eliminate federal income tax on those amounts but NOT payroll tax (FICA/SE tax). Tip and overtime income remains fully subject to Social Security tax (6.2% up to the wage base) and Medicare tax (1.45% + 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax if applicable). Do not tell a taxpayer these amounts are "tax-free" -- they are income-tax-deductible only.

> **TRAP:** The auto loan interest deduction applies only to new vehicles with final U.S. assembly, purchased with loans originated after December 31, 2024. Used vehicles, leases, and vehicles assembled outside the U.S. do not qualify. The taxpayer must include the VIN on the return. Refinanced loan interest qualifies only up to the original principal balance.

> **TRAP:** The OBBBA senior deduction ($6,000) is separate from and in addition to the existing additional standard deduction for age 65+ ($2,000 single / $1,600 per spouse MFJ in 2025). Do not confuse or conflate these two deductions. A single filer age 65+ taking the standard deduction in 2025 could potentially claim: $15,750 (base) + $2,000 (additional for age) + $6,000 (OBBBA senior) = $23,750 in total deductions before any other items.
