# Home Office Deduction -- California

*Last Edited: 2026-03-26*

**R&TC Section(s):** Sections 17201, 17024.5, 17076
**Parallel IRC Section:** IRC Section 280A
**California Conformity Status:** Partially Conforms -- California conforms to IRC Section 280A qualification requirements but does NOT conform to the TCJA/OBBBA suspension of employee unreimbursed business expenses (including home office). W-2 employees can still deduct home office expenses in California as miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the 2% AGI floor.

## Overview

California conforms to the qualification requirements for the home office deduction under IRC Section 280A. For self-employed taxpayers, the deduction works identically on both federal and California returns. The critical California difference applies to **W-2 employees**: federally, the TCJA eliminated the deduction for unreimbursed employee business expenses (including home office) for 2018--2025, and the OBBBA made this elimination permanent. California did **not** conform to either change. California employees may continue to deduct home office and other unreimbursed employee business expenses as miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to a 2% California AGI floor on Schedule CA (540), Part II.

## California Rule

### Self-Employed Taxpayers (Schedule C Filers)

1. **Qualification:** Conforms to IRC Section 280A -- the home office must be used regularly and exclusively as the principal place of business, as a place to meet clients/customers, or as a separate structure used in connection with the business
2. **Deduction computation:** Same as federal -- actual expenses (mortgage interest/rent, utilities, insurance, depreciation, repairs) allocated by square footage or rooms; or the simplified method
3. **Simplified method:** $5 per square foot, maximum 300 square feet, maximum deduction **$1,500** -- conforms to federal
4. **Schedule CA impact:** None for self-employed. Federal and California amounts are the same.
5. **Depreciation note:** Home office depreciation follows California's own depreciation rules (no bonus depreciation, lower Section 179 limit). If the home was depreciated using federal bonus or enhanced 179, an adjustment on Form 3885A is required.

### W-2 Employees -- CRITICAL CALIFORNIA DIFFERENCE

1. **Federal rule (2018--2025 TCJA; permanent under OBBBA):** The deduction for unreimbursed employee business expenses, including home office expenses, is **eliminated**. IRC Section 67(g) suspends all miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the 2% AGI floor. The OBBBA makes this permanent.
2. **California rule:** California does **not** conform to the TCJA elimination of miscellaneous itemized deductions. California employees **may** deduct unreimbursed employee business expenses, including home office expenses, as miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the **2% California AGI floor**.
3. **Where to claim:** Schedule CA (540), Part II, Line 19 ("Unreimbursed employee expenses: job travel, union dues, job education, etc."). The employee may also need to attach federal Form 2106 (computed using California amounts).
4. **2% AGI floor:** The total of all miscellaneous itemized deductions (Line 22) is reduced by 2% of California AGI (Line 24). Only the excess is deductible.
5. **Qualification requirement still applies:** The employee must still meet the IRC Section 280A tests -- the home office must be for the **convenience of the employer**, used regularly and exclusively for business, and there is no other suitable workspace available.

### Types of Deductible Employee Expenses (California Only)

California employees may deduct the following on Schedule CA (540), Part II, Lines 19--21, subject to the 2% AGI floor:

- Home office expenses (rent, utilities, depreciation, insurance)
- Job-related travel expenses
- Union dues
- Professional organization dues
- Job education expenses
- Work-related supplies
- Tax preparation fees (Line 20)
- Investment expenses (Line 21)
- Safe deposit box fees (Line 21)

None of these are deductible federally under OBBBA.

## Conformity Analysis

**IRC RULE:** OBBBA permanently eliminates the deduction for miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the 2% AGI floor, including unreimbursed employee business expenses and home office expenses for employees. Self-employed home office deduction under Section 280A remains available.

**CALIFORNIA RULE:** California does not conform to the OBBBA (or TCJA) elimination. Both self-employed and employee home office deductions are available. Employee deductions are miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to the 2% California AGI floor.

**Controlling Authority:** R&TC Section 17201 (block conformity to IRC as of January 1, 2025, but with explicit nonconformity to TCJA Section 67(g) and OBBBA changes); R&TC Section 17076 (California AGI definition); Schedule CA (540), Part II.

**Schedule CA Impact:** Employees claim unreimbursed business expenses (including home office) on Schedule CA (540), Part II, Line 19. This creates a **California-only subtraction** that reduces California taxable income below federal taxable income. No corresponding federal deduction exists.

## OBBBA Non-Conformity

The OBBBA makes the elimination of miscellaneous itemized deductions **permanent** (previously scheduled to expire after 2025 under the TCJA). California does **not** conform to this permanent elimination. Per the FTB 2025 Instructions for Schedule CA (540), California law does not conform to the OBBBA. The 2025 California 540 Booklet explicitly lists the OBBBA among federal acts to which California does not conform.

This means:
- **Pre-OBBBA expectation:** The TCJA suspension of miscellaneous itemized deductions was set to expire after 2025. Some taxpayers expected the federal deduction to return in 2026. The OBBBA eliminated this possibility.
- **California impact:** No change. California never conformed to the TCJA suspension, so the OBBBA's permanent extension has no additional California impact. California employees have been able to deduct these expenses continuously since 2018.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** When processing a return for a W-2 employee who works from home, **always check** whether the employee qualifies for the California home office deduction. The employee can claim this on Schedule CA even though no federal deduction is available. Ensure IRC 280A qualification tests are met.

> **AGENT RULE:** For self-employed taxpayers, the home office deduction is the same on both returns. No Schedule CA adjustment is needed unless the home office involves depreciable property (in which case, California depreciation rules apply per Form 3885A).

> **AGENT RULE:** The 2% AGI floor uses **California AGI**, not federal AGI. California AGI may differ from federal AGI due to conformity differences. Compute the floor using the California-specific amount.

> **AGENT RULE:** Employee transportation fringe benefits (parking, mass transit) are also deductible in California but not federally. If the employee has unreimbursed transportation fringe expenses, include them on Schedule CA (540), Part II.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Assuming employees cannot deduct home office expenses because the federal deduction was eliminated. This is true federally but **not** in California. California employees can still deduct these expenses on Schedule CA.

> **TRAP:** Applying the 2% AGI floor using federal AGI instead of California AGI. The floor is 2% of California AGI, which may be different from federal AGI. Always use the California-computed amount.

> **TRAP:** Failing to meet the IRC Section 280A qualification tests. Even though California allows the employee deduction, the home office must still be for the convenience of the employer, used regularly and exclusively for business purposes. A home office used "occasionally" or not required by the employer does not qualify.

> **TRAP:** Overlooking the Pease limitation (itemized deduction phaseout). California still imposes a phaseout on itemized deductions for high-income taxpayers (beginning at $252,203 single / $504,411 MFJ for 2025). This can further reduce the benefit of the home office deduction.

> **TRAP:** Depreciating the home office portion of the residence using federal bonus depreciation rates. California does not allow bonus depreciation. Home office depreciation must be computed using California MACRS rules (typically 39-year straight-line for the residential structure portion used as an office).

> **TRAP:** Employees who are required to work from home but whose employer provides an office they choose not to use may not qualify. The "convenience of the employer" test requires that the employer -- not the employee -- necessitates the home office arrangement. If an employer provides a suitable workspace but the employee voluntarily works from home, the deduction may fail the qualification test.

## Worked Example -- W-2 Employee Home Office

**Scenario:** A California W-2 employee with California AGI of $120,000 works from home 3 days per week at the employer's direction (no office space provided). Home is 2,000 sq ft; office is 200 sq ft (10%). Annual rent: $36,000. Utilities: $4,800. Internet: $1,800.

| Item | Allocation (10%) | Deductible |
|------|------------------|-----------|
| Rent | $3,600 | $3,600 |
| Utilities | $480 | $480 |
| Internet (business %) | $180 | $180 |
| **Total home office expense** | -- | **$4,260** |
| Other misc. itemized (tax prep, etc.) | -- | $500 |
| **Total misc. itemized deductions** | -- | **$4,760** |
| 2% AGI floor ($120,000 x 2%) | -- | ($2,400) |
| **Net California deduction** | -- | **$2,360** |
| **Federal deduction** | -- | **$0** (eliminated) |

The employee saves approximately $213--$258 in California state tax (at the 9.3%--10.3% marginal rate bracket) on a deduction that produces zero federal benefit. The Schedule CA (540), Part II entries create a California-only subtraction.
