# Business Meals and Entertainment Deductions -- California

*Last Edited: 2026-03-26*

**R&TC Section(s):** Sections 17201, 17201.1, 24343, 24443

## Overview

California generally conforms to the federal framework for business meals and entertainment deductions under IRC Section 274, with important nonconformities. Entertainment expenses are 100% nondeductible under both federal and California law (post-TCJA). Ordinary business meals remain 50% deductible under both systems. However, California does not conform to several TCJA and OBBBA modifications to employer-provided meals and employee fringe benefits, creating California-only deduction opportunities. The OBBBA's 2026 disallowance of employer convenience meals (Section 274(o)) does not apply in California.

## California Rule

### Entertainment Expenses

1. **100% nondeductible** -- California conforms to the TCJA disallowance of entertainment, amusement, and recreation expenses
2. This applies to: sporting events, concerts, theater, golf outings, club dues, recreational facilities
3. No separate-statement exception for food/beverages at entertainment events (must be separately invoiced and not extravagant)
4. The pre-TCJA 50% entertainment deduction is gone on both federal and California returns

### Business Meals -- Standard Rules

1. **50% deductible** -- California conforms to the standard 50% limitation on business meals
2. Requirements for deductibility:
   - The meal is not extravagant or lavish
   - The taxpayer (or an employee) is present at the meal
   - The meal has a clear business purpose
   - Costs of food and beverages are separately stated from any entertainment
3. California conforms to the five-element substantiation requirement:
   - Amount of each expense
   - Date and time of the meal
   - Place (name and location of restaurant/establishment)
   - Business purpose of the meal
   - Business relationship of the persons present

### Employer-Provided Meals -- CRITICAL OBBBA NONCONFORMITY (2026+)

1. **Federal rule (effective January 1, 2026 under OBBBA Section 274(o)):** Employer expenses for meals provided for the convenience of the employer or in employer-operated eating facilities are **100% nondeductible**. This reverses the prior 50% deduction.
2. **California rule:** California does **not** conform to OBBBA Section 274(o). FTB's Summary of Federal Income Tax Changes confirms: Section 70305 ("Exceptions from limitations on deduction for business meals") -- **No** conformity. R&TC Sections 17201.1, 24443.
3. **California treatment of employer-provided meals:**
   - De minimis fringe benefit meals (coffee, snacks, occasional meals): **100% deductible** in California
   - Employer convenience meals that do not qualify as de minimis: **50% deductible** in California
   - Meals provided on-premises for the employer's convenience under IRC Section 119: **50% deductible** in California
4. **Schedule CA adjustment (2026+):** Employers should claim a **subtraction** on Schedule CA for the difference between the federal disallowance (100%) and the California deduction (50% or 100% depending on classification)

### Employee Transportation Fringe Benefits -- TCJA Nonconformity

1. **Federal rule (TCJA, made permanent by OBBBA):** No deduction for employee transportation fringe benefits (parking, mass transit) paid by the employer
2. **California rule:** California does **not** conform. Employers may continue to deduct these expenses.
3. **Schedule CA adjustment:** Subtraction on Schedule CA (540), Line 3, for the amount of transportation fringe benefits disallowed federally

### Temporary 100% Meals Deduction (2021--2022) -- Not Applicable to California

1. The Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021 (Section 210) temporarily allowed a **100% deduction** for business meals provided by restaurants for amounts paid in 2021--2022
2. California did **not** conform to this temporary provision
3. California maintained the **50%** limitation during 2021--2022
4. For amended returns or carryover calculations from 2021--2022, California meals remain at 50%

## Conformity Analysis

**IRC RULE:** Business meals 50% deductible. Entertainment 100% nondeductible. Employer convenience meals (Section 274(o)): 100% nondeductible starting 2026. Transportation fringe benefits: nondeductible (permanent under OBBBA).

**CALIFORNIA RULE:** Business meals 50% deductible (conforms). Entertainment 100% nondeductible (conforms). Employer convenience meals: 50% or 100% deductible depending on classification (does not conform to Section 274(o)). Transportation fringe benefits: deductible (does not conform to TCJA/OBBBA).

**Controlling Authority:** R&TC Section 17201 (block conformity with exceptions); R&TC Section 17201.1 (explicit nonconformity to specific TCJA/OBBBA provisions including Section 274(o)); R&TC Section 24343 (CTL conformity to IRC Section 162); R&TC Section 24443 (CTL nonconformity to Section 274 modifications).

**Schedule CA Impact:** Subtraction on Line 3 Column B for: (1) employer convenience meals disallowed federally under Section 274(o) but deductible 50% or 100% in California; (2) transportation fringe benefits disallowed federally but deductible in California.

| Expense Type | Federal (2025) | Federal (2026+) | California (2025--2026) |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Standard business meals | 50% | 50% | 50% -- conforms |
| Entertainment | 0% | 0% | 0% -- conforms |
| Employer convenience meals (on-premises) | 50% | 0% (OBBBA 274(o)) | 50% -- does not conform |

## OBBBA Non-Conformity

1. **Section 70305 -- Exceptions from limitations on deduction for business meals:** OBBBA adds Section 274(o), disallowing 100% of employer-provided meals for the convenience of the employer and employer cafeteria meals, effective for amounts paid or incurred after December 31, 2025. Exceptions for restaurants that provide meals to employees and fishing vessels/facilities. California: **No** conformity. R&TC Sections 17201.1, 24443.

2. The OBBBA did **not** substantively change the 50% business meals deduction for client/prospect meals or the entertainment disallowance. These remain the same as under TCJA and are areas where California's conformity status is unchanged.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** Standard business meals with clients/prospects are 50% deductible on both federal and California returns. No Schedule CA adjustment needed for these expenses.

> **AGENT RULE:** For 2026+, employer-provided meals (cafeteria, on-premises, convenience meals) are 0% deductible federally but remain deductible in California (50% or 100% for de minimis). Compute the California subtraction on Schedule CA Line 3.

> **AGENT RULE:** Transportation fringe benefits (employer-paid parking, transit passes) are deductible in California but not federally. Always compute the California subtraction for these expenses.

> **AGENT RULE:** Apply the five-element substantiation test to all meal deductions. Missing any element can result in full disallowance on both federal and California returns. The substantiation requirement conforms.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Deducting entertainment expenses. Entertainment is 100% nondeductible on both federal and California returns. Do not confuse meals with entertainment. If food is provided at an entertainment event, the meal cost must be separately invoiced and reasonable to qualify for the 50% meals deduction.

> **TRAP:** Applying the temporary 100% restaurant meals deduction (2021--2022) to California. California did not conform. California meals were 50% during this period. Amended returns for 2021--2022 must use the 50% rate for California.

> **TRAP:** Assuming the OBBBA Section 274(o) disallowance applies to California for 2026. It does not. California continues to allow deductions for employer convenience meals and de minimis fringe benefit meals.

> **TRAP:** Failing to separately state food/beverage costs from entertainment. If meal costs are not separately invoiced from entertainment at the same event, the entire amount is treated as entertainment and is 100% nondeductible on both returns.

> **TRAP:** Overlooking the transportation fringe benefit nonconformity. Employers often add back parking and transit benefits for federal purposes and forget to subtract them for California. This is a relatively common and easy-to-miss California subtraction.
