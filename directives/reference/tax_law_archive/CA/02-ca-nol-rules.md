## Overview

California maintains its own NOL framework that differs materially from the federal rules under IRC Section 172. Key structural differences include a 20-year carryforward (vs. unlimited federal), generally no carryback (vs. limited federal carryback options), no 80% taxable income limitation (federal imposes this), and recurring legislative suspensions during budget shortfalls. SB 167 (June 2024) suspends NOL deductions for the 2024--2026 tax years for taxpayers with net business income or modified AGI of $1 million or more.

## California Rule

### Permanent Structural Rules

1. **Carryforward period:** 20 years (R&TC Section 17276(d)) -- compared to unlimited under federal post-TCJA rules
2. **Carryback:** Generally **not allowed** -- California repealed the 2-year carryback after tax year 2018
   - **Exception -- New businesses:** For tax years 2013--2018, NOLs incurred in the first 3 years of a new business could be carried back 2 years. This carryback is no longer available for current tax years.
3. **Percentage limitation:** California NOLs can offset **100%** of California taxable income (when not suspended) -- California does **not** conform to the federal 80% limitation under IRC Section 172(a)(2)
4. **Computation:** California NOL is computed using California income, deductions, and modifications -- not simply the federal NOL adjusted

### SB 167 NOL Suspension (2024--2026)

1. **Suspension period:** Tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2024, and before January 1, 2027
2. **Who is affected:**
   - **Individuals:** Suspended if **both** net business income **and** modified AGI are $1,000,000 or more
   - **Corporations:** Suspended if income subject to California taxation is $1,000,000 or more
3. **Below $1M threshold:** Taxpayers with net business income **or** modified AGI below $1,000,000 are **exempt** from the suspension
4. **Definitions:**
   - "Net business income" includes income from a trade or business (directly or through pass-through entities), rental activity income, and farming business income
   - "Modified AGI" is federal AGI determined without the federal NOL deduction
5. **Computation continues:** Taxpayers must continue to compute and carry over NOLs during the suspension period -- they simply cannot deduct them
6. **Disaster losses:** Disaster loss carryovers are **not affected** by the suspension

### Extended Carryforward for Suspended NOLs

| NOL Incurred In | Carryforward Extension |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Tax years beginning before January 1, 2024 | +3 years |
| Tax years beginning on/after Jan 1, 2024 and before Jan 1, 2025 | +2 years |
| Tax years beginning on/after Jan 1, 2025 and before Jan 1, 2026 | +1 year |

### SB 175 Revenue Trigger

1. The NOL suspension (and $5M credit cap) **will not apply** for 2025 and/or 2026 if:
   - The Director of Finance determines by the specified date that General Fund revenue over the multiyear forecast is sufficient without the revenue from the suspension, **and**
   - The annual Budget Act includes legislation specifying the suspension will not apply
2. Specified dates: May 14, 2025 (for TY 2025); May 14, 2026 (for TY 2026)
3. This determination is made separately for each tax year

## Conformity Analysis

**IRC RULE:** Federal NOLs have unlimited carryforward, optional 5-year carryback for farming losses, and an 80% of taxable income limitation on usage. OBBBA did not change the NOL framework.

**CALIFORNIA RULE:** 20-year carryforward, no carryback (currently), no 80% limitation (can offset 100%), but subject to periodic legislative suspensions. Currently suspended for 2024--2026 for taxpayers over $1M.

**Controlling Authority:** R&TC Sections 17276, 17276.24 (PITL suspension); R&TC Sections 24416, 24416.24 (CTL suspension); SB 167 (Ch. 35, Stats. 2024); SB 175 (revenue trigger).

**Schedule CA Impact:** When the NOL suspension applies, the taxpayer reports **no NOL deduction** on the California return even if a federal NOL deduction is claimed. The difference is an addition on Schedule CA. When the suspension does not apply (under $1M or post-2026), the California NOL deduction may differ from federal due to different NOL amounts, carryforward periods, and the absence of the 80% cap.

## Key Thresholds and Figures

| Item | Federal | California |
|------|---------|------------|
| Carryforward Period | Unlimited | 20 years (+extensions) |
| Carryback | 5-year (farming only) | None (currently) |
| Usage Limitation | 80% of taxable income | 100% (no cap, when not suspended) |
| Suspension (2024--2026) | None | Yes, if income >= $1M |
| Suspension Threshold | N/A | $1,000,000 (individuals: both net biz income AND mod AGI; corps: CA taxable income) |

## Section 461(l) Excess Business Loss -- California Does NOT Conform (As Written in Federal Law)

California enacted its own version of the excess business loss limitation under R&TC Section 17560.5, effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2018. Key differences from federal IRC Section 461(l):

1. California's limitation applies for tax years **beginning after December 31, 2018** -- it skipped federal TY 2018
2. California does **not** conform to the CARES Act suspension of IRC 461(l) for 2018--2020
3. California does **not** conform to the IRA extension of 461(l) through 2028
4. California does **not** conform to the OBBBA modification and permanent extension of 461(l) after 2026
5. Disallowed excess business losses are treated as **excess business loss carryovers** (not NOL carryovers as under federal law) per R&TC Section 17560.5(b)(3)
6. The threshold amounts are the same as federal ($305,000 single / $610,000 MFJ for 2024, indexed)
7. Computed on FTB Form 3461

### Three-Layer Federal Loss Stack vs. California

**Federal (2025):**
- Layer 1: Passive activity loss rules (IRC Section 469)
- Layer 2: Excess business loss limitation (IRC Section 461(l))
- Layer 3: 80% NOL limitation (IRC Section 172)

**California (2025):**
- Layer 1: Passive activity loss rules -- conforms, with modifications (rental real estate of real property businesses still passive)
- Layer 2: Excess business loss limitation -- California's own version under R&TC 17560.5 (different operative dates, no CARES/IRA conformity, carryover treated differently)
- Layer 3: **No 80% NOL limitation** -- California NOLs can offset 100% (when not suspended)

The California loss landscape is structurally different. The absence of the 80% NOL cap means losses that pass through Layers 1 and 2 can fully offset California income, whereas federal limits utilization to 80%.

## OBBBA Non-Conformity

The OBBBA (Section 70601) modifies and permanently extends the excess business loss limitation for noncorporate taxpayers (effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2026). California does **not** conform. R&TC Sections 17551, 17560.5, 24681.

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** For 2024--2026, check both the individual's net business income AND modified AGI. If **either** is below $1,000,000, the NOL suspension does **not** apply and the taxpayer can use NOL carryovers. Both thresholds must be met for the suspension to apply.

> **AGENT RULE:** Always verify the SB 175 revenue trigger status before computing the 2025 or 2026 California return. If the Director of Finance has determined General Fund revenue is sufficient and the Budget Act suspends the limitation, the NOL deduction is available regardless of income level.

> **AGENT RULE:** California NOLs can offset 100% of taxable income (no 80% cap). When modeling California tax liability, do not apply the federal 80% limitation to California NOL deductions.

> **AGENT RULE:** The excess business loss limitation under FTB 3461 uses California-specific rules under R&TC 17560.5, not federal IRC 461(l) directly. Disallowed amounts carry forward as excess business loss carryovers, not as NOL carryovers. Do not apply CARES Act or IRA modifications.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Applying the federal 80% NOL limitation to California. California has no such limitation. An NOL of $500,000 can fully offset $500,000 of California taxable income (when not suspended).

> **TRAP:** Assuming the NOL suspension applies to all taxpayers. Taxpayers with income under $1 million are exempt. For individuals, the test requires **both** net business income and modified AGI to be at or above $1 million.

> **TRAP:** Forgetting to extend the carryforward period for suspended NOLs. An NOL incurred in 2020 normally expires after 20 years (2040). With the 2020--2021 suspension (+2 years) and 2024--2026 suspension (+3 years), the expiration could extend to 2045.

> **TRAP:** Treating California excess business loss carryovers as NOL carryovers. Under R&TC 17560.5, they are separate -- different character, different carryover rules.

> **TRAP:** Failing to compute the California NOL independently. The California NOL starts with California taxable income, not the federal NOL. Depreciation add-backs, conformity differences, and other adjustments mean the California NOL amount is almost always different from federal.
