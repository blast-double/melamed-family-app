## Overview

California offers its own research credit under R&TC Sections 17052.12 (PITL) and 23609 (CTL), modeled on the federal credit but with lower credit rates and California-specific modifications. SB 711 (signed October 1, 2025) introduced the Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC) method starting for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2025, replacing the Alternative Incremental Research Credit (AIRC). Separately, SB 167 imposes a $5 million annual cap on business credits (including R&D) for 2024--2026. California independently allows immediate expensing of R&E expenditures, never having adopted the TCJA Section 174 amortization requirement.

## California Rule

### Credit Methods Available (Tax Years 2025+)

1. **Regular Credit Method:**
   - 15% of qualified research expenses (QREs) exceeding the base amount
   - 24% of basic research payments (in-house)
   - 12% of contract research expenses (65% of amounts paid for qualified research performed on behalf of the taxpayer in California; 75% for certain basic research)

2. **Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC) -- NEW under SB 711:**
   - Available for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2025
   - Credit equals **3%** of QREs that exceed 50% of the average QREs for the three preceding tax years
   - If the taxpayer has **no QREs in any one** of the three preceding tax years: credit equals **1.3%** of current-year QREs
   - Election is made on a timely filed original return (cannot be changed on amended return)
   - Revoking the ASC election in a later year requires FTB consent before filing the original return
   - See FTB Notice 2024-01 for revocation procedures

3. **Alternative Incremental Research Credit (AIRC) -- REPEALED:**
   - No longer available for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2025
   - Prior rates were: 1.49% / 1.98% / 2.48% on tiered incremental QREs

### QRE Requirements

1. Only research **conducted in California** qualifies (R&TC Section 23609(c)(2)(A))
2. QREs include in-house research expenses (compensation, supplies, computer use) and contract research expenses
3. The base amount cannot be less than 50% of current-year QREs (applies to both regular and ASC methods)
4. Start-up companies have a 10-year phase-in for the fixed-base percentage; the maximum fixed-base percentage is 16%

### California R&E Expensing -- Independent of Federal

1. California does **not** conform to TCJA Section 174 amortization (5-year domestic / 15-year foreign)
2. California does **not** conform to OBBBA Section 174A immediate expensing
3. California independently allows **immediate expensing** of all R&E expenditures, following IRC Section 174 as it read on January 1, 2015
4. Both domestic and foreign R&E costs are fully deductible in the year incurred for California purposes
5. This creates a favorable California adjustment for tax years 2022--2024 (when TCJA required federal capitalization) and aligns with federal for 2025+ (when OBBBA restored immediate expensing federally)

### Section 280C Election

1. Without the election: claim the full credit but reduce the R&E deduction by the credit amount
2. With the Section 280C(c)(2) election: take a reduced credit (approximately 79% of the gross credit at a 21% corporate rate) and keep the full R&E deduction
3. The election must be made on a timely filed original return and is irrevocable for that tax year
4. California follows the federal Section 280C rules with modifications for California credit rates

### Carryforward

1. Unused California R&D credits carry forward **indefinitely** until exhausted
2. Credits must be applied to the earliest taxable year possible
3. No carryback of R&D credits is allowed
4. Subject to the SB 167 $5M credit cap during 2024--2026 (see below)

## Conformity Analysis

**IRC RULE:** Federal R&D credit under IRC Section 41: 20% regular credit on QREs over base amount, or 14% ASC on QREs over 50% of 3-year average (6% if no prior QREs). OBBBA Section 174A restores immediate R&E expensing for domestic costs.

**CALIFORNIA RULE:** 15% regular credit / 24% basic research / 3% ASC (1.3% if no prior QREs). Immediate R&E expensing independent of federal -- always allowed. Credit subject to $5M annual cap (2024--2026).

**Controlling Authority:** R&TC Sections 17052.12, 23609 (credit); R&TC Section 17201 (R&E expensing conformity to pre-TCJA IRC 174); SB 711 Section 120 (ASC adoption, AIRC repeal); R&TC Section 17201.1 (decouples from TCJA 174 and OBBBA 174A).

**Schedule CA Impact:** For 2022--2024, a **subtraction** on Schedule CA Column C for the difference between federal amortized R&E and California fully expensed R&E. For 2025+, minimal difference (both federal and California allow immediate expensing of domestic R&E, but California also allows immediate expensing of foreign R&E while federal requires 15-year amortization).

## SB 167 Credit Cap (2024--2026)

1. For tax years 2024--2026, SB 167 caps total business credits at **$5,000,000 per year** for each taxpayer (or combined reporting group)
2. The R&D credit is **subject** to this cap
3. **Exempt** from the cap: low-income housing credit, elective pass-through entity tax credit
4. Credits disallowed due to the cap remain as carryovers with extended carryforward periods (extended by the number of years disallowed)
5. Under SB 175, taxpayers may make an irrevocable election for an annual refundable credit of 20% of the qualified credit that would have been allowed without the $5M limitation
6. **Revenue trigger:** The credit limitation may not apply for 2025 and/or 2026 if the Director of Finance determines by May 14, 2025 (for 2025) or May 14, 2026 (for 2026) that General Fund revenue is sufficient without it, and the annual Budget Act includes legislation to suspend the limitation

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** For any taxpayer with California QREs, compute the California R&D credit separately from federal. Use California-specific rates (15%/24%/3%). Only include research conducted **in California**. Federal QREs for out-of-state research do not qualify.

> **AGENT RULE:** When total business credits exceed $5M in 2024--2026, apply the SB 167 cap. Carry forward the disallowed portion. Check whether the SB 175 revenue trigger has been activated for the applicable tax year before applying the cap.

> **AGENT RULE:** For R&E expensing, California always allows immediate deduction regardless of federal treatment. For 2022--2024, this creates a California subtraction. For 2025+, domestic R&E aligns, but foreign R&E still creates a subtraction (California allows immediate expensing; federal requires 15-year amortization).

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Including out-of-state research in the California credit computation. Only research conducted **within California** qualifies for the California credit. Multi-state companies must allocate QREs by location.

> **TRAP:** Using federal ASC rates (14%/6%) for California. California ASC rates are 3%/1.3% -- significantly lower. This is a deliberate legislative choice, not a conformity issue.

> **TRAP:** Assuming the $5M credit cap does not apply because the business is small. The cap applies per taxpayer (or per combined reporting group), regardless of size. A startup with a large R&D credit in its first profitable year may hit this cap.

> **TRAP:** Failing to make the ASC election on a timely filed original return. The election cannot be made or changed on an amended return. Once the filing deadline passes, the method is locked.

> **TRAP:** Confusing the AIRC repeal with the ASC adoption. The AIRC is no longer available for 2025+. Taxpayers who previously used the AIRC must elect either the regular method or the new ASC.
