## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive reference for California Franchise Tax Board (FTB) and Employment Development Department (EDD) tax forms relevant to personal income tax, corporate/franchise tax, partnership/LLC tax, withholding, credits, and related administrative purposes. For each form: the form number, full name, purpose, who files, due date, and e-file availability are provided.

---

## California Rule

All forms listed are filed with the California Franchise Tax Board (FTB) unless otherwise noted. EDD forms are filed with the California Employment Development Department. Due dates assume calendar-year filers; fiscal-year filers use corresponding fiscal-year deadlines.

## Agent Rules

1. **Know the correct form for each entity type.** Partnerships file Form 565, LLCs file Form 568, S corporations file Form 100S, C corporations file Form 100. Do not confuse entity return types.

2. **Real estate withholding uses Form 593 — not Form 592.** Form 592 is for general nonresident withholding on services, rents, and distributions. Form 593 is exclusively for real estate transactions.

3. **PTE forms are three-part.** Form 3893 (payment voucher), Form 3804 (entity-level tax calculation), and Form 3804-CR (individual credit claim). All three must be filed for a complete PTE election.

4. **Schedule CA is the most commonly filed schedule.** Nearly every California taxpayer with any federal-to-California adjustment must file Schedule CA (540) or Schedule CA (540NR).

5. **Form 540X is paper-only.** Amended individual returns cannot be e-filed. They must be mailed to the FTB.

6. **EDD forms are separate from FTB forms.** DE 4, DE 9, DE 9C, and DE 542 are filed with the Employment Development Department, not the FTB.

---

## Common Traps

1. **Filing Form 540 instead of Form 540NR.** Nonresidents and part-year residents must file Form 540NR, not Form 540. Using the wrong form can cause processing delays and incorrect tax computation.

2. **Missing the March 15 deadline for pass-through entities.** Partnerships (565), LLCs (568), and S corporations (100S) have a March 15 due date — one month earlier than C corporations and individuals.

3. **Forgetting the $800 annual LLC tax (Form 3522).** This is due on the 15th day of the 4th month after the start of the LLC's tax year, separate from the LLC fee computed on Form 568.

4. **Not filing Form 3840 for out-of-state like-kind exchanges.** Taxpayers who exchange CA real property for out-of-state property must file Form 3840 annually until the deferred gain is recognized.

5. **Using Form 592 for pass-through entity withholding.** For withholding on entity owners (partners, members, shareholders), use Form 592-PTE, not Form 592.

6. **Overlooking Form 3853 (health coverage).** California has an individual shared responsibility requirement. Taxpayers without minimum essential coverage must file Form 3853 or face a penalty.
