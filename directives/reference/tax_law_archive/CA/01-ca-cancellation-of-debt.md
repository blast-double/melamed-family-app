## Overview

California generally conforms to the federal treatment of cancellation of debt (COD) income. Under both federal and California law, the cancellation or forgiveness of a debt results in gross income to the debtor equal to the amount canceled, unless a specific exclusion applies. California conforms to the major IRC 108 exclusions -- bankruptcy, insolvency, qualified farm indebtedness, qualified real property business indebtedness (QRPBI), and student loan forgiveness. California also conforms to the tax attribute reduction rules of IRC 108(b). The areas of non-conformity are narrow and primarily relate to timing elections under IRC 108(i) for business debt reacquisitions.

## California Rule

### General Inclusion

1. **COD income is includable in gross income.** When a creditor cancels, forgives, or discharges a debt (in whole or in part) for less than the full amount owed, the debtor realizes income in the amount of debt canceled. This conforms to IRC 61(a)(12).

2. **Reporting.** The creditor issues Form 1099-C when canceling $600 or more of debt. The debtor reports the income on the tax return unless an exclusion applies.

3. **Nonrecourse debt -- no COD income.** When nonrecourse debt is discharged through foreclosure or deed in lieu, the transaction is treated as a sale of the securing property for the full amount of the debt. There is no COD income -- only potential gain or loss on the disposition. California conforms to this treatment.

### Exclusions Under IRC 108 -- California Conformity Status

4. **Bankruptcy exclusion (IRC 108(a)(1)(A)) -- CONFORMS.** Gross income does not include COD income if the discharge occurs in a Title 11 bankruptcy case. The taxpayer must be under the jurisdiction of the bankruptcy court. California conforms via R&TC 24307 (corporations) and general conformity under R&TC 17024.5 (individuals).

5. **Insolvency exclusion (IRC 108(a)(1)(B)) -- CONFORMS.** COD income is excluded to the extent the taxpayer is insolvent immediately before the discharge. Insolvency is measured as liabilities exceeding assets (FMV). The exclusion is limited to the amount of insolvency. California conforms.

6. **Qualified farm indebtedness (IRC 108(a)(1)(C)) -- CONFORMS.** COD income from discharge of qualified farm debt by a qualified person (lender) is excludable if the taxpayer is a solvent farmer. California conforms.

7. **Qualified real property business indebtedness (IRC 108(a)(1)(D)) -- CONFORMS.** For non-C-corporation taxpayers, COD income from discharge of QRPBI is excludable. The exclusion is limited to the excess of the outstanding principal over the FMV of the real property (reduced by other debt on the property), and further limited to the aggregate adjusted basis of depreciable real property held before the discharge. California conforms.

8. **Student loan forgiveness (IRC 108(f)) -- CONFORMS (with multiple provisions).**
   - IRC 108(f)(1): Discharge of student loans contingent on working in specified occupations for specified periods -- excluded. California conforms.
   - IRC 108(f)(5): The ARPA (2021) temporarily excluded all student loan discharge from gross income for discharges occurring after December 31, 2020, and before January 1, 2026. California has provided its own exclusions through several R&TC provisions:
     - R&TC 17132.11: Exclusion for student loan debt canceled under income-based repayment programs (effective for discharges on or after January 1, 2014).
     - R&TC 17144.6: Exclusion for discharge of student loans of eligible individuals (for taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2019, and before January 1, 2024).
     - R&TC 17144.8: Exclusion for specific repayment plan discharges (Income Contingent, Pay As You Earn, Revised Pay As You Earn).
   - With SB 711 updating the conformity date to January 1, 2025, California now conforms to IRC 108(f)(5) for discharges through December 31, 2025.

9. **Purchase-money debt reduction (IRC 108(e)(5)) -- CONFORMS.** If a seller-financed debt is reduced by the seller, it is treated as a purchase price reduction (basis reduction) rather than COD income. California conforms.

10. **Deductible-if-paid exclusion (IRC 108(e)(2)) -- CONFORMS.** COD income does not arise if payment of the liability would have been deductible. California conforms.

### Attribute Reduction Rules (IRC 108(b)) -- Conforms

11. When COD income is excluded under the bankruptcy or insolvency provisions, the taxpayer must reduce certain tax attributes in the following order (unless the taxpayer elects to reduce basis first):
    1. Net operating losses (NOLs)
    2. General business credit carryovers
    3. Minimum tax credit
    4. Capital loss carryovers
    5. Basis of property
    6. Passive activity loss/credit carryovers
    7. Foreign tax credit carryovers

12. California conforms to this attribute reduction framework. The reduction is reported on Form 982. Note that the California attribute amounts may differ from federal (e.g., NOL amounts, capital loss carryovers) due to other conformity differences.

### Non-Conformity: IRC 108(i) -- Business Debt Reacquisition Election

13. **California does NOT conform to IRC 108(i).** IRC 108(i), added by the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) of 2009, allowed taxpayers to elect to defer COD income from the reacquisition of applicable debt instruments in 2009 and 2010, recognizing the income ratably over five years beginning in 2014. California never updated its conformity to include this provision. Any deferral elected federally under IRC 108(i) must be reversed for California purposes -- the full COD income is includable in the year of the reacquisition.

## Conformity Analysis

**IRC RULE:** IRC 61(a)(12) includes COD income in gross income. IRC 108 provides exclusions for bankruptcy, insolvency, qualified farm debt, QRPBI, student loans, and purchase-money reductions. IRC 108(b) requires attribute reduction when exclusions are claimed. IRC 108(i) allows deferral of business debt reacquisition income (2009-2010 only).

**CALIFORNIA RULE:** R&TC 17024.5 and R&TC 24307 conform to IRC 108, with the exception of IRC 108(i). The major exclusions all apply. Attribute reduction under IRC 108(b) conforms. Form 982 is used for both federal and California purposes (with California-specific attribute amounts).

**Controlling Authority:** R&TC 17131, 17131.4, 17131.8, 17132.11, 17144, 17144.6, 17144.8, 24307; SB 711 (Conformity Act of 2025).

**Schedule CA Impact:**
- If IRC 108(i) deferral was elected federally: Column B addition in the year of reacquisition (recognize full income); Column C subtraction in years federal deferred income is recognized
- Student loan exclusions: Generally no adjustment needed if both federal and California exclude the income; adjustment needed only if the California exclusion window differs from the federal window
- All other exclusions: No adjustment needed (California conforms)

## Agent Rules

> **AGENT RULE:** For each COD event, verify that the applicable exclusion existed in law BEFORE the January 1, 2025 conformity date. If the exclusion was enacted by a federal law signed after January 1, 2025 (e.g., by the OBBBA on July 4, 2025), California does NOT conform unless separate California legislation addresses it. Check the FTB Summary of Federal Income Tax Changes for confirmation.

> **AGENT RULE:** When applying the insolvency exclusion, calculate insolvency separately for California purposes. California NOL carryforwards and other tax attributes may differ from federal. Use California-specific asset and liability values if there are differences in basis or deferred tax amounts.

> **AGENT RULE:** If a taxpayer elected IRC 108(i) deferral for a 2009 or 2010 business debt reacquisition, reverse the deferral for California. Report the full COD income on the California return in the year of the reacquisition event. In the years the federal deferred income is recognized (2014-2018), subtract the federal recognition amounts on Schedule CA Column C.

> **AGENT RULE:** For student loan forgiveness, check both the federal IRC 108(f) exclusion AND the California-specific R&TC provisions (17132.11, 17144.6, 17144.8). The California provisions may provide exclusions even where the federal provision does not, or may have different effective dates.

## Common Traps

> **TRAP:** Assuming California conforms to every COD exclusion. While conformity is broad, the IRC 108(i) business debt reacquisition deferral is a specific area of non-conformity that can result in significant California tax in the year of the event, even when the income is deferred federally.

> **TRAP:** Student loan forgiveness timing mismatch. The federal ARPA exclusion under IRC 108(f)(5) expires for discharges after December 31, 2025. California's standalone provisions have their own expiration dates. For discharges after December 31, 2025, both the federal and California exclusions may lapse, making the forgiveness fully taxable at both levels unless new legislation is enacted.

> **TRAP:** Attribute reduction miscalculation. When reducing tax attributes under IRC 108(b), use California-specific attribute amounts. California NOL carryforwards, capital loss carryovers, and basis amounts may all differ from federal. Reducing the wrong amounts leads to errors in future years.

> **TRAP:** Failing to file Form 982 for California. Even when the federal and California treatments match (same exclusion, same attribute reduction), Form 982 should be filed with both returns to document the exclusion and attribute reduction. The FTB may request it on audit.

> **TRAP:** Nonrecourse debt confusion. On foreclosure of nonrecourse debt, there is NO COD income -- only a deemed sale. The gain is the difference between the debt amount and the property's adjusted basis. Do not report both COD income and a sale gain; it is one transaction.
