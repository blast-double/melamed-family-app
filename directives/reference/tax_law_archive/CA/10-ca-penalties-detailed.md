## Overview

This document provides an extended penalty reference expanding upon the estimated tax penalty overview in 08-ca-estimated-tax-penalties.md. California imposes a comprehensive set of penalties for various failures to comply with tax law, ranging from late filing and late payment penalties to accuracy-related, fraud, and preparer penalties. Each penalty has specific computation rules, applicable code sections, and potential defenses. The FTB's Manual of Audit Procedures (MAP Chapter 11) and the Penalty Reference Chart (FTB 1024) are the authoritative internal references.

## Reasonable Cause Defenses

Most California penalties can be abated (waived) if the taxpayer demonstrates "reasonable cause" for the failure. The burden is on the taxpayer to establish reasonable cause. Common reasonable cause defenses include:

### Late Filing / Late Payment (19131, 19132)
- Death or serious illness of the taxpayer or immediate family member
- Unavoidable absence (e.g., incarceration, natural disaster)
- Destruction of records by fire, flood, or other casualty
- Reliance on erroneous advice from the FTB
- Reliance on a competent tax professional (with documentation)
- Inability to obtain necessary records despite diligent efforts

### Accuracy-Related (19164)
- Reasonable basis for the position taken on the return
- Substantial authority for the tax treatment
- Adequate disclosure of the position on the return
- Good faith reliance on professional advice

### Estimated Tax (19136)
- Safe harbor exceptions (90% current year / 100%-110% prior year)
- Underpayment created by a law change enacted during the tax year
- Underpayment created by disallowance of a specific credit under Section 17053.80(g) or 23623(g)
- Casualty, disaster, or other unusual circumstances
- Retirement during the tax year (after age 62) and reasonable cause shown

### Fraud (19164(b)(1))
- **No reasonable cause defense exists for fraud penalties.** However, the FTB bears the burden of proving fraud by clear and convincing evidence.

## Interest Computation Rules

### General Rules
- **Rate:** Federal underpayment rate per IRC Section 6621 (federal short-term rate + 3 percentage points). Adjusted quarterly.
- **Compounding:** Interest is compounded **daily**, not monthly or annually.
- **Start date:** Interest runs from the original due date of the return (without extensions) to the date of payment.
- **No tolling during OTA appeals:** Unlike federal Tax Court proceedings, interest continues to accrue during the entire protest and OTA appeal process.
- **Overpayment interest:** The FTB pays interest on overpayments. The rate is generally the same as the underpayment rate for individuals; for corporations, the overpayment rate may be lower.

### Rate History (Recent Quarters)

Interest rates are published quarterly by the FTB based on the federal rate. Check the FTB website for current rates. The rate has typically ranged from 3% to 8% in recent years, depending on the federal funds rate environment.

## Supervisor Approval Requirement

Under R&TC Section 19187, the FTB must obtain **supervisor approval** before imposing any:
- Accuracy-related penalty (19164)
- Failure to furnish information penalty (19133)
- Fraud penalty

This approval requirement mirrors the federal IRC Section 6751(b) requirement and provides a procedural safeguard. Failure to obtain supervisor approval prior to the first written communication proposing the penalty may invalidate the penalty.

## Abatement Procedures

### Reasonable Cause Request
Taxpayers can request penalty abatement by:
1. Writing to the FTB with a detailed explanation and supporting documentation
2. Calling the FTB during business hours
3. Using MyFTB online account messaging
4. Responding to the penalty notice with written documentation

### One-Time Penalty Abatement (R&TC Section 19132.5)
As detailed in 08-ca-estimated-tax-penalties.md:
- Once-in-a-lifetime
- Applies to failure-to-file (19131) and failure-to-pay (19132) penalties only
- For taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2022
- Individual taxpayers only (not entities, estates, or trusts)
- Must be in compliance with all filing requirements
- Must have paid all outstanding liabilities or be current on an installment plan

### Installment Agreement
The FTB offers installment agreements for taxpayers who cannot pay the full liability. Penalties and interest continue to accrue during the installment period. A collection cost recovery fee is assessed ($362 for individuals/partnerships; $292 for corporations for periods starting July 1, 2025).

### Offer in Compromise (R&TC Section 19443)
The FTB may accept a reduced payment if there is doubt as to liability or collectibility. The OIC process is separate from the IRS OIC process.

## OBBBA Non-Conformity

The OBBBA does not make material changes to the federal penalty framework. California's penalty structure operates independently, and the OBBBA has no direct impact on California penalty computation. However, to the extent OBBBA changes alter the taxpayer's federal tax liability without corresponding California changes, the resulting California underpayment may trigger estimated tax penalties if not properly anticipated.

## Agent Rules

1. **Check for supervisor approval.** Any accuracy-related or fraud penalty must have documented supervisor approval before the FTB's first written communication proposing the penalty. Challenge penalties that lack this approval.

2. **Always evaluate reasonable cause.** Every penalty has potential reasonable cause defenses. Document the circumstances thoroughly before accepting or contesting a penalty.

3. **The one-time abatement is a powerful tool.** If the taxpayer has never used it, consider requesting the R&TC Section 19132.5 abatement for first-time filing or payment penalties.

4. **Interest is not a penalty and cannot be abated for reasonable cause.** Interest is statutory and accrues automatically. Only in very limited circumstances (FTB error causing delay) can interest be reduced.

5. **The demand penalty minimum ($14,600 for 2025) is severe.** Respond to FTB demand letters promptly. The demand penalty is in addition to late filing and late payment penalties.

6. **Track the fraud burden of proof.** The FTB must prove fraud by clear and convincing evidence. The burden does not shift to the taxpayer. Challenge fraud penalties vigorously when the evidence is insufficient.

---

## Common Traps

1. **Ignoring FTB demand letters.** The demand penalty ($14,600 minimum for 2025) is assessed in addition to other penalties. Failure to respond converts a manageable situation into a severe penalty.

2. **Not requesting the one-time abatement.** Many taxpayers do not know this option exists. Always check eligibility.

3. **Assuming penalties cannot be contested.** Most penalties have reasonable cause exceptions. Document the circumstances and request abatement.

4. **Confusing penalty abatement with interest abatement.** Interest cannot be abated for reasonable cause. Only penalties can be reduced or eliminated through reasonable cause or the one-time abatement.

5. **Missing the supervisor approval defense.** If the FTB failed to obtain supervisor approval before proposing the penalty, the penalty may be invalid. Request documentation of approval.

6. **Overlapping late filing and late payment.** When both apply, the computation interacts. Ensure the combined amount does not exceed the correct combined maximum.
